1. What you learned about Flex and CSS.

The Flexible Box Layout or Flexbox is a CSS model that enables the alignment of items within a single dimension which makes it a useful little toolbox to place divs inside divs. In this system it is possible to balance and build the whitespace more comfortably as opposed to traditional methods like the Float breaks. One can determine however the placement of other interior angles within the group by using, for example, justify-content, align-items and ‘flex-direction’ properties. Flexbox works extremely well in the creation of a mobile first website as resizing elements within the site such that they can be moved to occupy available space is very efficient with this option functioning which is especially suited for this method of website development.

Why do you need to have an accessible website? What does that mean?

Accessibility is crucial in designing an effective and friendly website. It simply implies that even users with disabilities can easily interact with and navigate through the given content. Accessibility helps make a website functional to persons with disabilities who might depend on such adaptive strategies as screen readers, or voice or keyboard active functions. Making content available to the largest possible audience is something users should aspire to when making a website. There are reasons that are practical to do so, such as the fact that many countries have implemented laws that require web sites to meet standards (such as the ADA in the U.S.) in terms of access. There is maximum support of all major search techniques is a classic example of catering for disabled users in site planning as well.
