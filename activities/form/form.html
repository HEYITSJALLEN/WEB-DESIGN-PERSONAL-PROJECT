The form tag has an "action" attribute. What does it do?

The action attribute in a form specifies the URL where the form's data will be sent upon submission.

The form tag has a "method" attribute. What does it do?

The method attribute in a form defines how the data will be sent to the server, typically as either POST or GET.

What is the difference between a "POST" and a "GET"?

The difference between POST and GET is that GET appends form data to the URL and is visible, while POST sends data in the request body, which is hidden from the URL and better suited for sensitive information.
